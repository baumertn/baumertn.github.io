I read _The School of Essential Ingredients_, a short novel by Erica Bauermeister.

It was recommended to me as „modern Persona games in book form“.
This assessment is corret in _some_ aspects.

The story centers around a cooking class that stretches over months.
But it is not the point.
It serves a regular meeting that connects the characters, who would not have meet and and stayed in contact.

The characters are of different ages – barely adults to seniors – and from different backgrounds.
Each one starts with their private problems.
They seem to be small, everyday problems but are reveald to be regret, loneliness, and missed changes – among others.

Through interaction between the characters these problems are gradually revealed.
And concludes in change in the characters.

It is a calm, contemplative story. Without need for dramatic crisis. That is very refreshing.

**Recommendation: Yes**
