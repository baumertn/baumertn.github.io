AI is _the_ hot topic right now. Everyone has opinions on it.
Some call it the future, others decry it. I fall closer to the latter camp.

Here I just want to write down my thoughts on the current state of AI.

The tech is amazing. I've not not grown up with the old text adventure games where you had to type
the commands and hope that the computer would understand it, but seeing how these systems
are able to parse instructions and "comprehend" them is a feat that I can not downplay.

Furthermore, it is a novel way to interact with a big chunk of knowledge on a surface level.

I limited my AI usage.

## AI for Coding
For coding I use [Supermaven](https://supermaven.com/) in Neovim as a fancy autocomplete – never generating more than one line at a time.
It is a very nice system for error message in particular. I am writing more Go recently and the need for explicit error messages is high.

AI seems to be "okay" for web development tasks, particularly frontend, from what I can see online.
In other fields the penetration seems lower.

I've tried to utilize AI with Ansible. For simple tasks or roles it works nicely. Anything that with increased complexity
had me intervene manually and fix things and cost more time.

That all said, I can see potential for boring refatoring tasks (that can't be done with even a complex search-replace) or
writing boilerplate code.

## AI for the Creative Arts
Generating funny images to share with friends seems to be the largest use case so far.
But the idea of comercilizing that feels off and given OpenAI's recent shutdown of Sora, the industry might see it the same?

I generally don't understand why it was pushed so hard in this direction.

I've used image generation occasionally to create inside jokes for my friends.
We did use some AI music tool to generate some stupid songs as well.
But both are just for fun in our friend group and we would not be sad if we could not have done that.

## AI for (Re)search
One of the original big goals of AI from (I think) OpenAI was to cure cancer.
I don't know how that is going, but it seems not a goal anymore.

I do use AI frequently as a replacement for web seraches.
This might be a larger indicator for the quality decline of modern web search engines
and the messed up world of SEO – in large part fuled by AI slop websites.

## AI in other Fields
AI is used in many more fields. But I do not have any experience in those. Making it hard to judge.

Early experiments to replace human customer service people with AI have been unsuccessful from what I've read.

So it appears to me that outsourcing jobs to AI is not yet feasible.

## Financial Situation
The financial situation around AI is crazy. So much money. How are they ever going to make profits to pay that back?
I understand that the goal is to build AGI and actually have all the money.
But the behaviour of these companies does not fill me with confidence that they will achieve AGI anytime soon.

Nvidia is probably going to end up the only lasting winner. As they were after the end of the main crypto hype.

I will not buy an Nvidia GPU as long as Jensen stays CEO. He has shown his colours.

## Fear of Replacement
Currently, I'm not afraid of being replaced by AI.
It is true that there have been layoffs in software development that have been attributed to AI.
This sucks, of course. But I'm unsure if it wasn't other justification for layoffs.
Tech companies have had figuretivly regular layoffs since Covid.


## My Ideal Future
I have recently started to explore AI locally on my machine with _Qwen 3.5_. Of course, I'm still figuring things out,
but so far I'm impresed how well this works on my consumer hardware (AMD Radeon RX 9070 XT).

I like to believe that local AI could be the future. Companies should push to reduce the hardware requirements of their models
and release them into the public. I'm not asking them to do that for free. The could release a smaller model for free personal use
and the larger ones under a commercial license.

From what I've seen in the tech world, invoative ideas are often coming from individuals trying things out.
